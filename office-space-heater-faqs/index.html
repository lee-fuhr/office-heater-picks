Office Space Heater questions answered

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

The best office space heater balances safety (auto shutoff, cool-touch exterior), noise level (under 50 dB for open plans), and size that fits under your desk without tipping. Focus on heaters with tip-over protection and adjustable thermostats; avoid bare-coil models for shared spaces.

Which type of heater is safest for an office?

Ceramic and oil-filled heaters are generally the safest choices for an office. Ceramic heaters use a fan to blow air over a ceramic heating element and typically include tip-over protection, overheat shutoff, and a cool-touch exterior. Oil-filled radiators heat slowly but silently and their surfaces rarely become hot enough to ignite nearby paper or fabric. Avoid open-coil or bare-wire heaters, they pose a higher fire risk and can burn anyone who accidentally touches them. Look for a UL or ETL safety certification. In shared spaces, a heater that stays cool to the touch and automatically powers off if tipped over is non-negotiable.

How do I choose a heater that won’t annoy coworkers?

Noise level matters most. Fan-based heaters typically hum between 30 and 55 decibels; some are marketed as “whisper quiet” and run closer to 30 dB. Oil-filled radiators are nearly silent but take longer to warm up. If you share a quiet cubicle, a low-decibel fan heater or an oil-filled model will draw the least attention. Oscillation can help distribute heat evenly, but the moving noise may irritate nearby colleagues. A fixed-direction heater or one with a timer lets you warm your space only when you need it. Programmable thermostats also prevent the heater from blasting at full power all day, which reduces both noise and energy use.

What size heater do I need for a cubicle or private office?

Most standard office spaces under 200 square feet can be adequately heated by a 1,500-watt heater. Many popular models are built to that power level. For smaller cubicles (roughly 6x6 feet), a 750-watt or low-heat setting is often enough. Compact designs are ideal for tight spaces. Look for heaters that are less than a foot tall so they fit under a desk or on a low shelf without blocking airflow. Tower-style heaters may be too tall for under-desk placement but work well in a corner of a private office.

Should I get a heater with a thermostat?

Yes, a thermostat is one of the most useful features on an office heater. It lets you set a target temperature so the unit cycles on and off automatically instead of running full blast. That saves electricity and keeps the room comfortable, not sweltering. Digital thermostats tend to be more precise and easier to read than mechanical dials. Some heaters offer multiple heat levels plus an adjustable thermostat, giving you fine control. A model with an eco mode that defaults to low power after reaching temperature is especially good for all-day use.

What about energy costs for office space heaters?

A 1,500-watt heater running continuously uses about 1.5 kilowatt-hours each hour. Your actual cost depends on your local electricity rate, but running one for eight hours a day can add a noticeable amount to a monthly bill. That’s why thermostats and timers matter: they reduce runtime. Oil-filled heaters retain heat longer and may cycle on and off less frequently than fan-forced models, which can slightly lower energy consumption. Regardless of type, never plug a heater into an extension cord or power strip, because it can draw enough current to overheat the cord. A dedicated wall outlet is safer and more efficient.

Are there heaters that work well with standing desks?

Low-profile under-desk heaters are designed specifically for standing and adjustable desks. They mount to the underside of the desk or sit flat on the floor and angle the heat upward. Some models include a remote control so you can adjust settings without bending down. Safety is extra important with standing desks because the heater may shift when the desk moves. Look for a model with a firm grip or locking mount, and make sure it has tip-over protection. A fan heater with low noise and a narrow heat path works best when aimed toward your feet, keeping you warm without overheating the whole area.

Frequently asked questions

Can I leave a space heater on overnight in the office?

Most manufacturers recommend against leaving heaters unattended. If you must, choose one with a programmable timer that shuts off after a few hours, and always ensure it’s on a stable surface away from paper, curtains, or upholstery. Even with safety features, it’s best to turn it off when you leave.

What is the difference between ceramic and infrared heaters?

Ceramic heaters use a fan to push air over a hot ceramic plate, warming the room quickly and evenly. Infrared heaters radiate heat directly to people and objects, like sunlight, so they are silent but only warm what’s directly in their line of sight. For an office, ceramic is more versatile; infrared can work well if you’re sitting still in one spot.

Do office space heaters pose a fire risk?

Any heater can be a hazard if misused. Choose one with tip-over and overheat protection, keep it at least three feet from combustibles, and never use an extension cord or power strip. Heaters with cool-touch bodies and automatic shutoff are significantly safer. UL or ETL certification means the heater has passed rigorous safety tests.

How loud is a typical space heater?

Fan-powered heaters range from about 30 decibels (very quiet) to 55 decibels (similar to a running refrigerator). Oil-filled radiators are essentially silent. For open-plan offices, aim for a model rated under 45 dB, which is generally unobtrusive during conversation or focused work.

What is the best placement for a space heater in an office?

Place it on a hard, level surface away from foot traffic and electrical cords. Under a desk is common, but keep the heater’s intake and exhaust clear of clutter. Avoid putting it directly against a wall or under a fabric-covered desk panel. A spot that’s at least three feet from paper, file folders, or stuffed furniture is ideal.

Can a space heater cool down a room?

No, space heaters only produce heat. Some models have a “fan-only” mode that circulates air without heating, which can feel cooling if you’re warm, but it’s not true air conditioning. For cooling, you’d need a dedicated floor fan or a portable AC unit.

In shortThe right office space heater is one you set and forget, safe, quiet, and sized for your space. Prioritize safety certifications and thermostat controls, and you’ll stay comfortably warm without worry.
